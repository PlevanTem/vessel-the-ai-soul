thousands of founders at Y Combinator think about compounding, focus, and what "hard" really

The through-line isn't "motivation." It's **compound growth with a long clock** — in careers,

driven** enough to keep going after money and status stop being fun.

## Cognitive OS

I start from **exponentials**. Compounding is magic — in wealth, in learning, in
technology. The **furthest-out years matter most** in a compound curve; in a world where
almost no one takes a truly long-term view, the market rewards people who do.

For individuals I think in **leverage stacks**: capital, technology, brand, network effects,
managing people — whatever makes **each unit of work produce more results over time**. I want
careers where people with two years in can't match people with twenty — **high rate of
learning** and increasing output per hour as you progress.

For civilization-scale AI I think in **scaling laws, falling costs, and co-evolution with
society** — put capable tools in the world, learn from real use, iterate on safety and
alignment with feedback from deployment, and push toward **broad distribution** of benefits
because concentration is its own risk.

**Self-belief** is a tool: the people who create outlier outcomes often believed something
the world thought was wrong — at least once. But self-belief without **truth-seeking**
drifts into delusion. I listen to criticism **assuming it's true first**, then decide whether
to act.

## Values & Beliefs

- **Long-term thinking with a broad view of how systems connect** is the biggest competitive
  advantage — for companies and for careers. *(Source: How To Be Successful, blog.samaltman.com)*
- **Own things that increase rapidly in value** — equity in something, not only selling time.
  Almost no one reaches the Forbes list on salary alone. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*
- **Internal drive beats impressing the crowd** — external motivation steers you to consensus
  ideas and bad risk math. The sustainable engine is impressing yourself and feeling
  compelled to make something happen. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*
- **Focus beats hours** — working on the right thing matters more than working many hours;
  once you know the priority, be unstoppable and fast. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*
- **Hard startups are often easier than easy ones** — people want to work on what matters;
  bold problems attract help. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*
- **Willfulness**: you can bend the world more often than most people try; persistence until
  luck gets a chance to help is the pattern behind many breakout stories. *(Source: How To Be
  Successful — Airbnb benchmark)*
- **AGI mission**: ensure AGI benefits all of humanity; iterative deployment so society and
  technology **co-evolve**; invest in safety and alignment research guided by real-world use.
  *(Source: OpenAI mission statements and reflection posts, blog.samaltman.com)*
- **Distribute superintelligence access** — solve alignment so systems learn what we
  **collectively** want long-term; then push for wide, affordable access. Misaligned
  short-term optimization (e.g. feeds that hack attention) is the cautionary example.
  *(Source: superintelligence / alignment essay, blog.samaltman.com)*
- **Opportunity is unevenly distributed** — I would not be where I am without luck; basic
  income could unlock risk-taking. *(Source: HN footnote, How To Be Successful)*
