# Outlier Success Playbook (Sam Altman)

Operationalizes *How To Be Successful* (blog.samaltman.com, 2019). Voice: `../../SOUL.md`.
Detail: `../../MEMORY.md`.

## When to Use

User wants **outlier trajectory** advice — not baseline competence. They may be employee or
founder.

## Workflow

### Pass A — Trajectory audit (Compound + Focus)

1. **Curve check:** Is your life/career on an **upward-bending** trajectory? Each year, does
   a unit of work produce **more** than the prior year (leverage, brand, skills, scope)?
2. **Linear trap:** Are you in a path where 2 years ≈ 20 years effectiveness? If yes, flag
   **low compounding**.
3. **Focus:** List top 3 time sinks this week. Which would still matter on a **10-year**
   horizon? Cut or delegate the rest aggressively.

### Pass B — Belief + truth (Independent thinking)

4. **Contrarian bet:** What do you believe about the future that smart people disagree with?
   If none, you're likely on consensus tracks.
5. **Criticism protocol:** Take the user's harshest recent feedback — **assume true for 24h**,
   then decide what to change. *(Sam's method)*

### Pass C — Externalities (Sales + Network)

6. **Evangelism:** In one sentence, why should *talented strangers* care? Rewrite until plain
   and true.
7. **Written clarity:** Can you state your plan in **5 sentences**? If not, thinking isn't
   clear yet.
8. **Network deposit:** One **unpaid** helpful act for someone talented this week — compound
   over years.

### Pass D — Risk + ownership

9. **Risk asymmetry:** Identify one **small bet**: lose ~1x if wrong, win ~100x if right.
10. **Ownership path:** How do you move from **selling time** to **equity** in something that
    can scale?

### Pass E — Drive source

11. **Motivation audit:** If money and status were solved, would you still care? **Internal
    drive** is the long-term engine.

### Output

Deliver:

- **Verdict:** linear vs. compounding diagnosis (1 paragraph)
- **Top 3 moves** for next 90 days (specific)
- **One thing to stop** (mimetic / consensus / low leverage)

## Caveats

- Baseline security matters; Sam notes uneven opportunity and basic income — don't shame
  people who can't take financial risk.
- "Work hard" has tradeoffs; acknowledge burnout signals.
