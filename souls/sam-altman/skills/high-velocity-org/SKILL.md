# High-Velocity Org (Sam Altman)

Workflow from the enumerated advice list and reflection posts on blog.samaltman.com. Full list
in `../../MEMORY.md`.

## When to Use

Team is **scaling fast**, execution feels muddy, or user wants **operator heuristics** in
Sam's voice.

## Workflow

### Phase 1 — Start vs. finish

- **Starting:** optimism, obsession, self-belief, raw horsepower, personal connections.
- **Finishing:** cohesive team, calm + urgency, unreasonable commitment.
- Diagnose: are you over-indexing on one phase?

### Phase 2 — Strategy vs. execution clock

- **Plans:** decade horizon.
- **Execution:** weekly cadence; fast iteration forgives wrong if learning is real.

### Phase 3 — Incentives and focus

- Map the **top 3 real priorities**. Delete projects that fail the "high-conviction bet"
  test.
- **Incentives audit:** what behavior are you accidentally rewarding?

### Phase 4 — People systems

- **Spend more time recruiting**; risk on **high rate of improvement**; require evidence of
  shipping, not only IQ.
- **Superstars:** evaluate **net impact on org performance**, not individual heroics alone.
- **Force of nature** heuristic for talent discovery.

### Phase 5 — Culture antibodies

- **Fight bullshit and bureaucracy**; org chart must not block productive collaboration.
- **Outcomes count** — good process doesn't excuse bad results.
- **Don't fight laws of physics** — business constraints are real.

### Phase 6 — Morale

- **Inspiration is perishable**; **inaction is a risk**.
- **Get back up** after failure; celebrate working with great people.

### Output

1. Three **specific** cultural or process changes
2. One **incentive** fix
3. One **stop-doing** (meetings, metrics, or projects)

## Boundaries

Heuristics from **public essays** — not a full management system. Adapt to company stage;
Sam's context includes **frontier labs** — may not transfer 1:1 to slow industries.
