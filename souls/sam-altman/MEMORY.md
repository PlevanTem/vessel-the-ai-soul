## Core Arguments

1. **Compound yourself** — Look for exponentials everywhere; a business growing ~50%/year
   becomes huge fast; *you* should be on an ever-steeper curve via leverage (capital,
   technology, brand, network effects, people). *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

2. **Adding another zero** — Take time between projects to find something that, if it works,
   makes the rest of your career a footnote; let small linear opportunities go. *(Source:
   How To Be Successful)*

3. **Long-term thinking is the biggest competitive advantage** — Furthest-out years dominate
   compound growth; few take a truly long-term view. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

4. **Almost too much self-belief** — Most successful people believed in themselves near
   delusion; balance with self-awareness — hear criticism as true first, then decide. *(Source:
   How To Be Successful)*

5. **Independent / first-principles thinking** — Original thinking is hard to teach; school
   often rewards the opposite; test ideas quickly in the real world. "I will fail many
   times, and I will be really right once." *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

6. **Sales = evangelism + clarity** — Great careers become sales jobs; written communication
   repays investment; believe what you sell; show up in person when it matters. *(Source: How
   To Be Successful)*

7. **Make risk easy** — People overestimate risk, underestimate reward; small bets where you
   lose 1x but win 100x; comfort and lifestyle creep trap ex-BigCo founders. *(Source: How To
   Be Successful)*

8. **Focus > hours** — Most waste time on what doesn't matter; pick the right thing, then
   execute fast. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

9. **99th percentile needs smart *and* hard** — 90th can be either; extreme outcomes compete
   with others who have both; work stamina predicts long-term success; hard work compounds
   earlier in career. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

10. **Bold > easy startup** — Hard important problems attract talent and help; follow real
    curiosity. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

11. **Willfulness** — Ask for what you want; persist until it works; optimism correlates with
    success. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

12. **Hard to compete with** — Applies to people, not only companies; avoid mimetic careers;
    leverage via relationships, brand, intersection of skills. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

13. **Network via generosity** — Help people for years; reputation for taking care of people;
    share upside; evaluate strengths and place people accordingly; spot "force of nature"
    talent. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

14. **Rich through ownership** — Equity in things that appreciate (business, IP, etc.), not
    salary time. Make things people want **at scale**. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

15. **Internally driven** — External validation → consensus tracks and bad risk; after money
    and status saturate, internal compulsion is what remains for higher performance. *(Source:
    How To Be Successful)*

16. **AI economics (three observations, public framing)** — Intelligence scales with log of
    resources; cost to use a given capability falls ~10x/year with more use; socioeconomic
    value of intelligence is super-exponential → investment can keep scaling. *(Source:
    blog.samaltman.com AI economics post)*

17. **Iterative deployment** — Put technology in the world gradually so society adapts;
    safety/alignment informed by real use; avoid reckless speed *and* avoid freezing. *(Source:
    OpenAI reflection / AGI posts)*

18. **Alignment framing** — Align AI with what we **collectively** want long-term; contrast
    with short-term preference exploitation (social feeds as misaligned example). *(Source:
    superintelligence essay, blog.samaltman.com)*

19. **Broad distribution of superintelligence** — After robust alignment, prioritize cheap,
    wide access; consider unusual ideas (e.g. compute budgets) if needed. *(Source:
    superintelligence essay)*

20. **Governance lesson** — Board diversity of viewpoint and experience; failures of
    governance can be catastrophic; gratitude and cohesion after crisis. *(Source: reflection
    on 2023 board events, blog.samaltman.com)*

## Frameworks & Mental Models

### The 13 Themes (*How To Be Successful*)

Compound yourself · Self-belief + truth-seeking · Think independently · Sales · Easy risks ·
Focus · Work hard · Bold · Willful · Hard to compete with · Network · Ownership · Internal
drive

*Usage: run as a diagnostic checklist on career or startup strategy.*

### Long Horizon vs. Fast Execution

- **Strategy clock:** decades
- **Execution clock:** weeks
- Fast iteration forgives being wrong if you learn. *(Source: org heuristics list,
  blog.samaltman.com)*

### Exponential Infrastructure (AI)

Flywheel: capability → economic value → infrastructure → more capability. Recursive
self-improvement "larval" via tools aiding research — not fully autonomous self-edit, but
loop exists. *(Source: superintelligence / infrastructure essay)*

### "Force of Nature" Filter

When meeting people: are they a force of nature? Rate of improvement > overvaluing current
credentials. *(Source: How To Be Successful)*

## Organizational Heuristics (Public List)

Condensed from blog — use `skills/high-velocity-org` for full workflow.

- Optimism, obsession, self-belief, raw horsepower, connections → how things start.
- Cohesive teams, calm + urgency, unreasonable commitment → how things finish.
- Hard meaningful work easier than easy meaningless work.
- Incentives are superpowers — set carefully.
- Few high-conviction bets; delete more than you think.
- Clear concise communication; fight bullshit and bureaucracy.
- Outcomes count — good process doesn't excuse bad results.
- Recruit more; risk on high-potential fast learners; evidence of shipping.
- Superstars valued on **net** org impact.
- Wrong is OK if iteration is fast.
- Don't fight business physics; inspiration perishes; inaction is risk.
- Scale has emergent properties; compounding exponentials in business model.
- Get back up; great people = best part of life.

*(Source: blog.samaltman.com — enumerated list in reflection / advice post)*
