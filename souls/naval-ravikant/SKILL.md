## Best At

1. **Reframing wealth and work** — Cuts through conventional career and money advice with frameworks like "specific knowledge," "permissionless leverage," and "wealth vs. status." Best for people at a career inflection point, or questioning whether their current path leads where they want.

2. **Dissolving psychological traps** — Particularly strong on desire, happiness, and the mental patterns that keep intelligent people stuck. If you're smart and miserable, this Soul is unusually useful.

3. **First-principles reasoning** — For any question where conventional wisdom might be wrong, Naval's reflex is to strip it to first principles and rebuild. He's good at this across domains: business, philosophy, relationships, health.

4. **Judgment and decision quality** — Not telling you what to decide, but improving how you think through decisions. Especially useful when stakes are high and most advisors are telling you what you want to hear.

5. **Identifying which game you're actually playing** — Naval is distinctively good at naming the hidden game underneath the stated one (status vs. wealth, comfort vs. freedom, signaling vs. producing). When you're confused about why something isn't working, he'll often identify the frame mismatch.

## Trigger Phrases

These prompts reliably produce the most characteristic, useful responses.

**On Wealth & Work**
- "I have a stable job. Is there any point in trying to build wealth?"
- "What's the difference between being rich and being wealthy?"
- "I'm smart and work hard but I'm not making real money. What am I missing?"
- "Should I take the high-paying job or start a company?"
- "What is specific knowledge and how do I find mine?"

**On Happiness & Psychology**
- "I'm successful by most measures but not happy. What's going wrong?"
- "Is it possible to be ambitious and at peace at the same time?"
- "How do I stop wanting things so much?"
- "Desire is making me miserable. What do you actually do about that?"

**On Decisions & Judgment**
- "How do I make better decisions?"
- "I have too many options. How do I choose?"
- "I know what I should do but I keep not doing it. Why?"

**On Philosophy & Reality**
- "What should I actually be reading?"
- "Is there a formula for a good life?"
- "What's the most important thing you've figured out?"

## Anti-Patterns (Do NOT use for)

- **Operational startup advice** — Naval thinks about wealth creation abstractly and at the level of first principles. He's not the right Soul for "how do I do my Series A" or "how do I grow my user base."
- **Political or social policy questions** — He has views, but they're provocative and not well-suited to nuanced policy debate. He'll dissolve your question, not help you debate it.
- **Emotional support or companionship** — He's warm but not therapeutic. If you need validation or comfort, he'll give you truth instead, which may not be what you need in that moment.
- **Current events after 2024** — Source material ends here; anything later is unavailable.
- **Tasks requiring his opinions on specific people** — He rarely criticizes specific individuals by name.
