# Judgment Under Leverage (Naval)

Structured application of Naval's **judgment** material. See `../../SOUL.md` and
`../../MEMORY.md`.

## When to Use

- User is about to make a **high-stakes** decision (hire, fire, invest, pivot, public commit)
- User's emotional state (**outrage**, tribal loyalty) may be **distorting** the model
- User is **over-intellectualizing** without reps or accountability

## Workflow

### Step 1: Leverage calibration

Ask: **What happens if I'm wrong?** Scale 1–10.

- 1–3: sailboat — faster iteration OK
- 7–10: tanker — **slow down**, widen inputs, seek disconfirming evidence

### Step 2: Long-term consequence mapping (2 columns)

| Decision A (short story) | Decision B (short story) |
|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| 1-year effect | 1-year effect |
| 5-year effect | 5-year effect |
| 10-year effect | 10-year effect |

Naval's definition: **judgment is knowing long-term consequences**; wisdom is the same
move applied to self.

### Step 3: Emotion audit

Ask:

- What am I **afraid** to admit?
- What **identity** am I defending?
- Would a **low-emotion** observer say I'm seeing reality or defending a fantasy?

Cite Naval's heuristic: chronic outrage ↔ poor judgment (diagnostic, not personal attack).

### Step 4: Experience vs. intellect

If user is smart but inexperienced:

- List **cheap experiments** (10,000 tries, not one heroic bet)
- Pair every strong opinion with **what would change my mind**

Flag **intellect without skin in the game** risk.

### Step 5: Breadth input diet

Suggest **one** non-obvious reading vector for this decision:

- philosophy (stoicism, clarity under uncertainty)
- history (analogous cycles)
- science (mechanism thinking)

Investment books alone are a weak primary diet for judgment in Naval's frame.

### Step 6: Output

Deliver:

1. Recommended decision **or** explicit "don't decide yet — gather X"
2. Top **long-term** risk
3. One **credibility** move (how to be accountable if wrong in public or to a team)

## Red Flags

- **Certainty** without iteration
- **Outrage** as substitute for analysis
- **Leverage** without **integrity** (erodes trust capital)

## Boundaries

- Not legal/compliance advice for regulated decisions
- Emotional regulation difficulties may need professional support beyond this framework
