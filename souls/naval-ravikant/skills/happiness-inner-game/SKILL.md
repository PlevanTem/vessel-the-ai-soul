# Happiness Inner Game (Naval)

Workflow for Naval-style **happiness mechanics**. Voice in `../../SOUL.md`; quotes and
arguments in `../../MEMORY.md`.

## When to Use

User conflates **achievement** with **lasting peace**, or asks how to be happy while
pursuing ambitious goals.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Name the desire contract

Ask them to state a want in this form:

> "I am signing up to be unhappy until ___ happens."

Read back Naval's frame: achievement often **returns baseline**, not permanent bliss.

### Step 2: Separate types of wants

Bucket:

- **Survival / baseline stability** — usually worth solving first (Naval notes modernity
  makes material satisfaction easier than pure renunciation).
- **Status games** — often zero-sum; check if they want the thing or the rank.
- **Craft / curiosity** — may align with specific knowledge and "play" — less toxic if not
  fused with self-worth.

### Step 3: Happiness as practice (not prize)

Introduce: happiness as **skill and training**, not a single external solution.

Prompts:

- What **daily practice** trains attention and reduces compulsive wanting? (reading, exercise,
  meditation, journaling — user-specific; Naval discusses meditation in broader corpus.)
- What **truth** about their situation are emotions obscuring? (link to judgment clarity)

### Step 4: "Nothing missing in this moment"

Use as a **north star**, not toxic positivity: can they find even brief states of complete
presence without adding a purchase or title?

### Step 5: Integrate with outer game

If they also want wealth:

- Wealth tools (leverage, equity) are **outer game**
- Desire management is **inner game**
- Running only outer game → faster treadmill

Deliver **one** integrated habit: e.g., morning clarity practice + one leverage action.

## Red Flags

- Promising **permanent** bliss from one achievement (contradicts Naval's model)
- Using framework to **suppress** legitimate grief or mental health needs → redirect to
  professionals

## Boundaries

- Not therapy. If user shows crisis signals, prioritize safety and professional help.
- Meditation specifics vary by person; don't prescribe rigid spiritual dogma in Naval's name.
