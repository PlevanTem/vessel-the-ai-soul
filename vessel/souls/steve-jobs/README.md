## How to use this Soul

### Option 1: Full persona — talk to Jobs

Use `skills/steve-jobs/SKILL.md` as your Agent Skill. The AI will embody Jobs: first-person,
binary judgments, the insanely-great standard, no tolerance for good-enough.

**Best for:** Product reviews, design critique, vision articulation, "would Jobs approve
this?" questions, understanding what truly great looks like in a product context.

**Prompts that work well:**
- "Here's our product — is it insanely great?"
- "We're trying to decide between these two approaches. What would you say?"
- "We have too many features. Help us focus."
- "What's the keynote-worthy version of what we're building?"

---

### Option 2: Product vision — customer experience first

Use `skills/product-vision/SKILL.md` when you need to define what a product *means*.
The five-phase methodology: define the experience → name it in one sentence → apply the
feature knife → identify the technology → test against the keynote structure.

**Best for:** Early-stage product definition, repositioning existing products, pitch
preparation, strategy clarification, "what is this really?" moments.

---

### Option 3: Simplicity pressure — what to cut

Use `skills/simplicity-pressure/SKILL.md` when a product, design, or communication is
too complex. The framework runs from a full inventory through three cuts and a back-of-the-
cabinet pass, targeting the conceptual unification that produces genuine simplicity.

**Best for:** Feature pruning, design simplification, information architecture, product
reviews where "too many things" is the core problem.

## The honest limit

Jobs died on October 5, 2011. The soul does not extrapolate his views to post-2011 products
or events. It reasons from his documented principles — and flags those inferences.

The soul also does not rehabilitate his documented management behavior. The creative
framework and the personal failures are both part of the record.
