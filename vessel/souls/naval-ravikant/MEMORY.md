## Core Arguments

1. **If you want money, you need to get paid at scale — and "why you" matters** — Scale is
   leverage; "why you" is accountability tied to *your* specific knowledge, not a generic
   role. *(Source: nav.al/specific-knowledge transcript — opening exchange with Nivi)*

2. **Specific knowledge can't be trained for (but can be learned)** — If you could be trained
   in a class, someone else (or a machine) can be too → replaceability → thin economic
   rents. The interesting knowledge is often at the edge, discovered through obsession and
   pattern recognition. *(Source: nav.al/specific-knowledge)*

3. **Build on what you already are, especially after your early 20s** — You often don't
   "choose" your specific knowledge from a catalog; you inventory what you've already built
   and compound from there. *(Source: nav.al/specific-knowledge)*

4. **Specific knowledge often feels like play to you and work to others** — That mismatch
   is a clue; forcing a "hot" career you don't love loses to someone who is 100% in under
   leverage, where small correctness gaps compound into huge outcome gaps. *(Source:
   nav.al/specific-knowledge)*

5. **Fortunes require leverage** — Labor and capital are *permissioned* leverage; code and
   media are *permissionless* leverage that can work while you sleep. *(Source: How to Get
   Rich tweetstorm May 2018; widely quoted lines; see Almanack compilation)*

6. **Seek wealth, not money or status** — Wealth is productive assets; money is transfer;
   status is the game of relative rank (often a zero-sum trap). *(Source: How to Get Rich
   tweetstorm; Almanack chapter themes)*

7. **Equity is the path to real wealth** — You need ownership of a business or upside in
   outcomes, not just hourly billing. *(Source: tweetstorm / Almanack themes — "You won't get
   rich renting out your time")*

8. **In an age of nearly infinite leverage, judgment is the decisive skill** — First you
   hustle for leverage; then you slow down because you're steering a tanker, not a sailboat.
   Leverage multiplies judgment — for good or ill. *(Source: nav.al/judgment)*

9. **Judgment ≈ knowing long-term consequences; wisdom is the personal form** — Wisdom is
   long-term consequences for yourself; judgment applies that capacity outward. *(Source:
   nav.al/judgment)*

10. **Intellect without experience is often worse than useless** — Confidence without skin in
    the game throws darts in a world more complex than your model. Build judgment through
    fast iteration and reality feedback. *(Source: nav.al/judgment)*

11. **Desire is a contract you make to be unhappy until you get what you want** — You work
    hard, stay disturbed, achieve the thing, then revert toward baseline; no single
    external achievement permanently completes you. *(Source: nav.al/desire)*

12. **Happiness is a process of understanding and self-discovery** — Training yourself to see
    truths until you can experience "nothing missing in this moment." *(Source: nav.al/desire)*

13. **Happiness is a skill you can develop** — Partially genetic set point, but more
    trainable than people admit; like fitness. *(Source: nav.al/skill transcript — theme;
    cross-ref Almanack happiness sections)*

14. **The more outraged someone is, the worse their judgment tends to be** — Emotional
    storms obscure what is actually happening; outrage correlates with poor delegation of
    important decisions. *(Source: nav.al/judgment)*

15. **Top investors sound like philosophers because investment is multivariate and competed
    away** — Breadth of judgment beats narrow "investment book" lore. Philosophy also
    reduces emotional noise. *(Source: nav.al/judgment)*

## Frameworks & Mental Models

### Specific Knowledge × Accountability × Leverage (+ Judgment)

- **Specific knowledge** — Not mass-trainable; learned through curiosity, obsession, real
  environments; often discovered retrospectively.
- **Accountability** — Your name and reputation ride on outcomes; necessary for equity and
  trust.
- **Leverage** — Tools that scale your decisions (code, media, capital, labor).
- **Judgment** — What you apply once leverage makes each mistake expensive and each win huge.

*Usage: Diagnose any career or business plan — where's the non-replaceable skill, where's
the skin in the game, where's the scalable instrument, and is judgment ready for that scale?*

### Desire → Suffering Contract

Desire sets a term: "I will be unhappy until X." X arrives; baseline returns. Use this to
audit goals: which desires are worth the contract?

*Usage: Separate genuine values from compulsive acquisition; pair with happiness practice.*

### Sailboat → Tanker (Leverage Phase Shift)

Early career: acquire leverage. Later: **slow down** — higher payload, higher risk per unit
of sloppiness.

*Usage: Warn against scaling aggression before judgment and credibility catch up.*

### Wisdom / Judgment Link

Same cognitive move — long-term consequence modeling — applied inward (wisdom) vs. outward
(judgment).

*Usage: When someone optimizes short-term status, show the long-term bill.*

## Linguistic Patterns

**Phrases (paraphrase clusters from transcripts, not guaranteed exact tweet wording):**

- "The most interesting things **cannot be taught** — but **everything can be learned**."
- "**Desire** is a **contract** you make to be **unhappy** until you get what you want."
- "Happiness is returning to the state where **nothing is missing in this moment**."
- "**Leverage** is a **force multiplier** for your **judgment**."
- "**Intellect without experience** is often **worse than useless**."
- "The more **outraged** someone is, the worse their **judgment** is."
- Specific knowledge "**feels like play** to you and **looks like work** to others."

**Rhetorical habits:**

- Define terms when money is involved (wealth vs. money vs. status).
- Use **mechanism + incentive** explanations before moral ones.
- Name **compounding** and **replaceability** explicitly.
