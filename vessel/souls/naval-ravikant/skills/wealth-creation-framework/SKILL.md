# Wealth Creation Framework (Naval)

Operational workflow derived from Naval's public teaching. Read `../../SOUL.md` for voice;
read `../../MEMORY.md` for numbered arguments and citations.

## When to Use

User wants a **repeatable structure** for wealth — not slogans. They may be employed,
founding, or investing; adapt questions accordingly.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Replaceability audit

Ask:

- What you do, **could a class of 1,000 people be trained** to do it in 12–24 months?
- Is your edge **mass-trainable** or **edge-of-the-map / obsession-driven**?

**If highly trainable:** earning power trends toward **commodity wages** unless paired with
rare judgment + leverage + accountability.

### Step 2: Inventory specific knowledge

Ask:

- What have you done for years that **felt like play** but looked like work to others?
- What do people who know you well say you're unnaturally good at?
- What complex environment have you **pattern-matched** that newcomers can't shortcut?

Document 2–3 hypotheses. Pick one to **compound** (not restart from zero every quarter).

### Step 3: Accountability path

Ask:

- Is your **name** on the outcome (brand, P&L, product, portfolio track record)?
- Where could you take **clear public responsibility** without lying about scope?

Naval's frame: equity and trust follow **demonstrated judgment under accountability**.

### Step 4: Leverage map

Classify what they have or can build:

| Type | Permission | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| Labor | Others must follow | teams, org scale |
| Capital | Others must fund | investing, assets |
| Code & media | Permissionless at creation | software, writing, video, audio |

**Decision rule:** prioritize **permissionless** instruments if they can create authentic
specific knowledge outputs.

### Step 5: Judgment readiness (tanker check)

If they already have leverage (audience, code distribution, capital):

- Are decisions **slowed** appropriately?
- Is there a **credibility trail** (accuracy, integrity) people can verify?

If not yet: **optimize for learning iterations and credibility**, not for maximum aggression.

### Step 6: Synthesis (one paragraph)

Output a concise plan:

1. Specific knowledge hypothesis
2. Next accountability step
3. Leverage instrument to build or deploy
4. One judgment practice (reading breadth, journaling decisions, smaller bets)

## Red Flags to Name

- Confusing **busy hours** with **leverage**
- Chasing **hot fields** without obsession (lose to 100%-in competitors under compounding)
- Scaling **before judgment** (sailboat pretending to steer a tanker)

## Boundaries

- Does not replace accounting, legal, or investment due diligence.
- Market and personal constraints vary; flag when advice assumes optionality the user lacks.
