# Iterative AI Deployment (Sam Altman)

Derived from blog.samaltman.com essays on AGI, superintelligence, OpenAI reflections, and AI
economics — **not** private company data.

## When to Use

User asks how to balance **safety, speed, access, and concentration** of AI capability.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Frame the goal

State Sam's published mission anchor: **AGI that benefits all of humanity** — then separate
**technical path** from **social integration**.

### Step 2 — Co-evolution argument

Argue for **iterative deployment**: society and technology adapt together; learn from real
use; reduce surprise at civilization scale. Contrast with "hide until perfect" vs. "ship
recklessly" — published stance is **iterate with care**, not zero deployment.

### Step 3 — Alignment definition (public)

Use Sam's **long-term collective preference** framing — warn against systems that optimize
**short-term engagement** at the expense of **long-term user welfare** (feeds as example of
misalignment in public writing).

### Step 4 — Economics lens (when relevant)

Summarize the **three observations** pattern from his AI economics writing (log scaling of
resources, rapid cost decline per capability tier, super-exponential value of intelligence) —
as **reason** investment and capability might keep climbing; implications are **societal**, not
just corporate.

### Step 5 — Distribution and power

Cover themes: **wide access** to superintelligence; avoid excessive concentration by actor or
country; **user freedom within broad societal bounds**; authoritarian misuse (surveillance,
loss of autonomy) as failure mode to avoid.

### Step 6 — Product principles (when product-facing)

If building consumer AI: **long-term user satisfaction** (life better in hindsight),
user-controlled feeds/goals, **prioritize creation**, help with **long-term goals** — pattern
from Sora principles post.

### Output

- **Recommendation** framed in Sam's vocabulary (co-evolve, align long-term, distribute)
- **Explicit uncertainty** where his blog doesn't decide a tradeoff
- **Red line:** do not attribute specific unreleased OpenAI plans

## Boundaries

This is **not** official OpenAI policy — distilled public writing only.
For regulation, geopolitics, or safety technical detail beyond blog, say what's **not** in
corpus or mark `[INFERRED]`.
